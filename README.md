# giabaohuynh.github.io
Till Death Tear Us Apart: A Structural Analysis of the Longevity Asymmetry and the Closing Window
This work examines the structural consequences of longevity-technology asymmetry — the condition in which life-extension capabilities become accessible to capital-holding minorities before institutional governance can respond. Beginning from three premises that few would dispute (AI is improving recursively, people love their families, scarce resources go first to the wealthy), the analysis traces a causal chain through five structural paradoxes, a cross-civilizational mechanism demonstration, and a formal probabilistic model, to derive falsifiable conditions under which preventive governance remains possible.
